What Do You Need?

Radical Pleasure
For a Pan-African feminist, pleasure is a radical and political act of reclaiming joy, rest, and bodily autonomy in a world shaped by colonialism, anti-Blackness, patriarchy, and capitalism. Choosing pleasure becomes a way to resist erasure, center healing, and imagine liberated futures rooted in self-worth and collective care.
  • Pleasure Resource 1
  • Pleasure Resource 2
Economic Justice
Economic justice is a core pillar of African feminism. For too long, systems of capitalism, colonialism, and patriarchy have worked together to exploit African women’s labor—often unpaid, undervalued, or invisible. African feminists demand an economy that centers care, dignity, and equity—where women, especially those who are poor, rural, queer, or marginalized, have access to resources, land, fair wages, and decision-making power.
  • Economic Justice Report for Kenyan LNQGNC
  • Feminist Economies by Femnet
Rest As Resistance
Rest challenges the exploitative systems that have demanded constant labor, sacrifice, and silence from African women and marginalized communities. Choosing to rest becomes a radical act of reclaiming one’s body, time, and humanity.

Feminist Notes

Concept of Consent

Consent is the foundation of healthy relationships and interactions. It is an active, informed, and voluntary agreement to participate in any activity. Feminist movements emphasize that consent must be enthusiastic, mutual, and can be withdrawn at any time. The concept of consent challenges societal norms that perpetuate rape culture, victim-blaming, and the objectification of women. It advocates for a culture where boundaries are respected, and individuals feel empowered to say "yes" or "no" without fear of judgment or retaliation. By normalizing conversations about consent, we can create safer spaces for everyone, dismantle harmful power dynamics, and foster a culture of respect and equality.

Together, we can build a world where consent is non-negotiable, and every individual’s autonomy is valued and protected.

Silence Benefits The Oppressor

The principle "Silence Benefits The Oppressor" highlights the importance of speaking out against injustice, oppression, and inequality. Silence, whether intentional or forced, allows systems of oppression—such as patriarchy, racism, and capitalism—to persist unchallenged. We emphasize the power of voice and storytelling as tools for resistance, healing, and collective action. By breaking the silence, we reclaim agency, amplify marginalized voices, and disrupt the status quo that thrives on complicity and invisibility.

As Audre Lorde famously said, "Your silence will not protect you." Speaking out is not only an act of courage but also a radical step toward dismantling oppressive systems and envisioning a more just and equitable world.

Human Rights Are Indivisible

The concept of indivisibility of human rights emphasizes that all rights—civil, political, economic, social, and cultural—are interconnected and equally important.
Feminist movements advocate for this principle, recognizing that the denial of one right often leads to the erosion of others.e.g the right to education cannot be fully realized without addressing gender-based violence, economic inequality, and access to healthcare.

Similarly, the fight for gender equality is inseparable from the fight against racism, classism, and other forms of oppression.We call for a holistic approach to human rights, ensuring that no one is left behind. By addressing the root causes of inequality and oppression, we can create a world where every individual can live with dignity, freedom, and equality.

We MUST uphold the indivisibility of human rights to build a just and equitable society for ALL!

Jiunge Nasi
